ACE 2000 is an unassuming computer of custom make, most closely resembling early IBM Personal Computer models. Its frame is made of a durable grey plastic, and the attached keyboard and mouse seem to be wholly non-anomalous in nature, resembling keyboards and mice from roughly the same timeframe. Its screen seems to be higher resolution than expected, and primarily displays a different color, a neon shade of cyan, although it is also capable of displaying colored images, and playing high definition stereo sound.

ACE 2000 was discovered through an anonymous tip to check a seemingly unused computer in the back corner of the 3rd floor of an office building in Victoria, Canada. That segment of the office building had not been used in a while, although sources outline that the computer was likely not present at the time of the previous users of the space leaving. The computer did not contain any files upon opening.

ACE 2000 seems to possess the ability to store an unlimited amount of folders and files, and also seems to be completely immune to both anomalies (such as reality benders) and anti-anomalies (such as reality anchors), and no other device has been found to contain these properties, with attempts to recreate such a device with similar properties always failing.
